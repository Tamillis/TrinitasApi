## Magic

This is a magic module for Crescens, providing rules and Powers that enable fantasy magic.

Magic is categorised into five distinct disciplines known as Spheres:
- **The Arcane** - which relies on logical formulas, adjusting variables on the fly and the manipulation of raw magical force or other fundamental energies.
- **The Divine** - Faith magic which channels the power of divinity - be those personal Gods, forces of the Universe or something else, through devotion and divine fiat.
- **Witchcraft** - the Occult which involves bargaining with hidden or outer entities, unconvering corrupt rituals and daring to utter words of power.
- **Verdance** - primal magic which taps into the natural flow and raw power of life
- **Elementalism** - the magic of the planet - air, earth, fire, water and metal.

### Casting Magic
A character can power their magic in one of two ways: using their Mind (Intelligence) or using their Will (Spirit).

They then cast magic using one of four Skills (which add to the base list of skills): Arcana, Faith, Witchcraft and Verdance.

### Magic Powers
Characters gain access to magic by learning Magic Powers - each of which denotes a different school of magic they become able to employ to create magical effects.

Each power notes what magic Casting it uses (Mind vs Will), what skill (Arcana, Faith, Witchcraft, or Verdance) - and a list of kinds of effects at certain **Magnitudes**.

These Magnitudes correspond to the Rank in the skill used by that Magic Power. The better your skill in a sphere of magic - the more you're able to manipulate the magic you know into complex and/or powerful effects.

*Note*: a player and GM may decide a particular character is channeling a particular Magic Power differently, and dictate a different Stat and/or Skill to use - so long as they employ the Power accordingly.

#### Mana
Intelligence casting treats magic as a finite resource of calculated formulas. It uses a Mana pool equal to the character's Intelligence die max. Casting an effect costs Mana equal to its Magnitude. This Mana is reliable, recovering fully per Rest.

#### Strain
Spirit casting is powering magic with one's raw life force. Instead of using Mana, such casters risk Strain.

After manifesting an effect, a Spirit caster must roll their Spirit die plus their casting Skill against a Target Number (TN) dictated by the Magnitude of the effect + the number of spells the character is currently maintaing including this one. For example, the first Magnitude 3 spell a character casts with Will is a TN of 4. If they cast a magnitude 5 spell while maintaining 3 other spells - that's a TN of 9.

On success or failure, the spell effect occurs.

Failure results in a Strain Injury of a Rank equal to the Magnitude (appropriately described). These injuries can represent mental fatigue, back-fire burns, neural scarring or other more esoteric blow-back effects. They apply their Rank as a negative modifier to all subsequent Will casting rolls.

Strain Injuries are non-fatal by default.

**Spirit Burn** - alternatively, before rolling, a character can opt to "spirit burn", dropping their Spirit die a step down the chain to automatically succeed a Strain check. Their Spirit returns to normal after a Rest.

### Magnitude Calculation
To help gauge if a magical effect's magnitude, use these tables to generate Modifiers and add them up on a base cost of 1 (rounding up).

Modifier | Range
--- | ---
+0 | Touch or Self.
+0.5 (per target) | Target up to 30ft
+1 (per target) | Target up to 60ft
+2 (per target) | Target up to 100ft
-1 (per target) | Require a To Hit check vs the target's evasion.
+2 | Small Area: 10ft radius / 15ft cone / 30ft x 5ft line
+3 | Large Area: 30ft radius / 30ft cone / 60ft x 10ft line

Modifier | Potency
--- | ---
+0 | Utility: Light, sound, minor movement, +1 to a skill roll, 1 Damage Attack.
+1 | Standard: Healing (instant remove 1 Injury Rank OR remove Fatal), apply / remove 1 Round condition on a target (Stun/Blind/Immobilise...), d4 Damage Attack.
+2 | Powerful: Superior Healing (remove d3 Injury Ranks AND remove Fatal), apply / remove condition on a target Per Fight (Stun/Blind/Immobilise...), d8 Damage Attack.. +2 to a skill.
+4 | Extreme: Remove Injury completely, d12 damage, total transformation, reviving the recently dead. +3 to a skill

Modifier | Duration
--- | ---
+0 | Instantaneous: The effect happens and ends.
+2 | Sustained: Lasts as long as your focus (per fight).
+3 | Daylong: Lasts until next Rest.
-1 | Concentration: either of Sustained or Daylong can be reduced with Concentration - where if it is broken by another character's skill check, the spell ends early - and other concentration-requiring spells cannot be done.
+4 | Semi-Permanent: The change is physical or fixed in reality until the power source fades or a long time passes such as a Downtime

## Magical Combat
When a magical effect is used as an attack, the caster rolls their Stat plus their Casting Skill against the target's Defence. On a success, the caster rolls for damage to generate an Injury.

### Resisting Magic
A character can make a check vs the magic's magnitude as a TN to "resist" damage (reducing it to a minimum of 1 by the value the TN was exceeded by) or conditions (i.e. if they're affected or not).

Magical damage types are suggested per Power. Arcane magic typically deals Force or Lightning damage. Faith magic deals Light or Fire damage. Witchcraft often manifests as Dark, Psychic, or Poison damage. Verdance and Elementalism often utilizes Cold, Fire, or physical elements like Bludgeoning (Stone) or Slashing (Wind).

## Powers and Progression
Magic is unlocked through Powers. These do not grant individual spells but rather provide authority over a thematic domain. A character with the Pyromancy power can manifest any fire-based effect they can imagine, provided they have the Skill Rank to meet the required Magnitude.

Training in magic follows the standard progression rules of Crescens. Increasing a casting skill or a stat requires in-fiction time and dedicated practice. As a character's skill Rank grows, their ability to handle higher Magnitude effects increases, allowing them to naturally progress from simple sparks to city-leveling infernos.
