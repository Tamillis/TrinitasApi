# Module: Road Apocalypse

This is a Crescens module to play games set in a "Road Apocalypse"; where the world has ground down, dried out, and burned up over generations of neglect, abuse and greed until that was left was the road, the sun, and whoever was tough enough to still be moving. Water is worth more than blood, fuel is power, and your name (if you've earned one) travels faster than you do. Road Apocalypse is for games set in the wasteland: rust-plated, dust-choked, and furious to be left in a burnt up world.

This module adds combat Skills, systems for vehicles, chases & manoeuvres, scarce resources, barter, and reputation.

## New Skills

Road Apocalypse replaces Core's **Weaponry**, **Shooting**, and **Martial Arts** skills with the following:

- **Handguns** - pistols, revolvers, and anything fired one-handed at close quarters.
- **Rifles** - bolt-action, semi-auto, and lever-action long arms built for range and accuracy.
- **Shotguns** - spread-shot weapons of any configuration. To wasteland folk, they are all shotguns..
- **Machine Guns** - submachine guns, assault rifles, light machine guns, and any fully automatic spray.
- **Heavy Weaponry** - rocket launchers, grenade launchers, flamethrowers, and mounted crew-served weapons.
- **Melee** - clubs, knives, machetes, hatchets and other hand-to-hand tools of violence.
- **Throwing** - grenades, molotovs, pipe bombs, and anything else hurled with intent.
- **Brawling** - wasteland close-quarters combat. Fists, headbutts, choke holds, and the kind of fighting that ends on the ground. Replaces Martial Arts in name and flavour.

#### Weapon Tags
- **Brace** - if you do not brace to fire (going prone, setting up a tri-pod, anchoring on a wall etc.), you do so at a Disadvantage.
- **Concealable** - easily hidden, whereas other weapons are always obvious.
- **Explosive** - any Damaged targets are knocked Prone.
- **Heavy** - it is difficult to move quickly or long distances with it, needing two hands to operate without a Disadvantage.
- **Reliable** - opposite of unreliable, less likely to break. An advantage on any maintenance or wear-and-tear rolls.
- **Reloading** - weapons with the Reloading tag cost an Action to reload.
- **Terrifying** - causes a Damaged target to make a Roll or become Frightened.
- **Unreliable** - on a critical failure, a roll of 1, the weapon jams. An Action is needed to unjam it.

### Scrap

Scrap is the currency of making and fixing things. It is found, looted, and stripped from the environment.

Scrap can carry tags describing its nature, so note not just how much scrap you gather, but what kind. 

- **Pristine** - high quality, counts as 2 regular Scrap for any purpose
- **Hi-Tech** - electronic or precision components; required for advanced and exotic mods
- **Mechanical** - engine parts, gears, hydraulics; required for vehicle repairs and mechanical mods
- **Chemical** - fuels, solvents, reactive materials; required for fire and explosive mods
- **Bulk** - large structural pieces; required for vehicle armour and heavy construction

Tables are encouraged to define their own kinds of scrap, and apply different types of scrap in different situations.

## Scarce Resources

In the wasteland, necessities are never easy to come by.

### Water, Food, and Fuel

These three resources are tracked as **uses** on whatever container or supply holds them.

**Water** is the tightest clock. A character who goes a full day without water gains an **Exhaustion** Injury Rank, with each further day adding 1 Rank.

**Food** is a slower pressure. A character who goes two days without food gains an Exhaustion Injury Rank, with each further two days adding 1 Rank.

Eating and drinking can remove 1 Rank of such exhaustion per day.

**Fuel** runs out like any other resource. Without fuel, a vehicle is Stationary and, of course, will not start. Most vehicles use 1 jerry can of fuel a day.

A running vehicle that reaches its final fuel gains the condition **Running Dry** - the engine will stop the moment those uses are spent, and everyone can hear it choking. The clock is ticking.

### Scavenging

Finding water, food, and fuel should feel like a small victory. Most Food, Water and Fuel will need more than a simple successful Wilderness or Search roll. Characters will have to work the terrain, other wastelanders and hunt for secret stashes to yield the loot.

#### The Dry Spell

When crossing hostile terrain, the GM may declare a **Dry Spell**. The sun is high and hot, and the horizon is far and empty. Scavenging rolls are one TN step harder, and Water uses cost double.

### Item Tiers

All goods and services fall into one of five tiers: 
- **Dust** - Broken junk, common scraps, information no one needs. Worse than scrap, that can at least be re-used.
- **Scrap** - Useful junk, salvagable material, crude weapons, simple black powder, and edible "food" and "pottable" water
- **Trade Goods** - Medicine, quality tools, weapons, clean water, actual food, drums of oil
- **Rare Goods** - hi-tech scrap, personal armour, powerful weapons, bulk water and food supply, a cistern of processed fuel
- **Legendary Loot** - working pre-apocalypse vehicles, hi-tech weapons caches, clean water sources, fertile farmland, keys to vaults

### Barter
Society is gone but trade isn't. Everything has a price; the argument is what that price is.

When terms are not immediately agreed, make a **Barter Roll**. It is a social roll against the trader's relevant Stat Average as the TN, or as an Opposed Roll if they are actively negotiating back and forth.

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Critical Failure (natural 1) | Bad deal - cheated, offended, or robbed |
| Failure | Poor deal - their terms, their price |
| Success | Good deal - your terms |
| Decisive Success (by 4+) | Great deal - one tier better in your favour |

## Factions & Reputation
A faction is any group with shared interests and a collective memory: a raider band, a settlement council, a trading convoy, a cult. 

Player characters will track their **Reputation** with each Faction, which functions just like a Skill with a rank, only it can be positive *or* negative. Like a Skill, it is applied to relevant social rolls, but with that faction.

> Davito has *Dustwalker Cartel: +2*, which grants him a +2 on a Barter Roll he's making with one of their dealers. Then Johnny Crash walks into, and Davito scrambles to diffuse his aggression to prevent a fight, but makes the Persuasion roll at -3 because of his *Iron Road Gang: -3* reputation. A fight breaks out.

## New Statistic: Renown
The wilderness relies on more than just a character's body and wiles, but the reputation that procedes them. Road Apocalypse characters have one more Stat: **Renown**.

**Renown** is an optional new Statistic representing a character's legend in the wasteland.

Renown uses the standard dice chain (d4 to d12) and is derived like other stats, just use another dice.

A d4 means nobody has heard of you. A d12 means your name empties rooms or fills them with hope, depending on who's listening.

Renown can be used in place of any Statistic where reputation is the operative force - first impressions, Intimidation at distance, commanding strangers, Barter. It also appears in some Road Apocalypse Power prerequisites (so if you're not using it, change those as appropriate).

## Vehicles

Vehicles are tools and weapons, prized possessions of wastelanders. Often, they represent life.

Each vehicle has a **Frame** value, its structural Knock Out Limit. When total Damage ranks meet or exceed Frame, the vehicle is Wrecked.

Vehicles note their crew, fuel capacity and if they have Armour (which works just like character armour), and any cargo capacity. Vehicles will have vehicle tags for special rules that apply to them.

Vehicles in combat and in Chases (see Chases below) can undergo Manoeuvres (see Manoeuvres below).

### Vehicle Tags

- **Good Handling** - an Advantage on manoeuvre rolls
- **Poor Handling** - a Disadvantage on manoeuvre rolls
- **Fast** - changing speed costs a Bonus Action rather than an Action
- **Flying** - this vehicle flies.
- **Slow** - an old beater on its last legs, or a scrapped-together engine lacking power
- **Heavy** - a large vehicle often with great cargo or passenger capacity, and room for heavy weapon load-outs
- **Efficient** - uses half a jerry can a day.
- **Inefficient** - uses two (or more, if stated) jerry cans a day.

### Speed
When driving, vehicles do more than just Move like a character, they **Speed Up** and **Slow Down**, being in various states of "**Speed**".

Most vehicles go in one of three Speeds. It costs a driver an **Action** to change speed by one degree, as they Speed Up or Slow Down.

- **Stationary** - Not moving
- **Cruising** - Normal travel speed.
- **Top Speed** - Flat out. Fast and dangerous.

Heavy or Slow vehicles need time to get up to cruising speed, so have to go through one more stage between Stationary and Cruising:
- **Rolling** - slowly getting up to speed or coming to a stop

When travelling long distances, vehicles use their Cruising Speed.

### Vehicle Damage & Wrecked

Vehicle Damage works exactly like character Injuries: a descriptor, a rank, and a penalty to relevant rolls.

| Damage Rank | Descriptor Examples |
| -- | -- |
| 1 | Dented Panel, Cracked Mirror, Loose Fitting |
| 2 | Blown Tyre, Cracked Windscreen, Fuel Leak |
| 3 | Damaged Suspension, Buckled Frame, Engine Knock |
| 4 | Structural Failure, Severed Brake Line, Engine Fire |
| 5 | Frame Collapse, Catastrophic Fuel Loss, Rolled-over |
| 6 | Total Destruction - instantly Wrecked |

When total Damage ranks meet or exceed **Frame**, the vehicle is **Wrecked**. Out of combat, it could be repaired or scrapped for parts.

### Vehicle Repair
Vehicles don't have a Recovery Roll. Instead, during a Rest, a character can make a Crafting check with an amount and kind of Scrap appropriate to the vehicle at hand (GM call), defaulting to 1 scrap per damage rank, to remove 1 rank. Multiple characters can work on a vehicle at once, and if a character foregoes Resting, can attempt such a vehicle repair Crafting roll once per hour. 

### Stalled Condition

Most Conditions (stunned, blinded etc.) don't apply to vehicles, but they can Stall:

**Stalled** - the engine is dead. The vehicle cannot Speed Up, instead automatically Slowing Down at the end of a Round, unless a character spends an Action and succeeds on a medium Roll (TN 4) to restart it.

## Chases and Manoeuvres

Chases in Road Apocalypse are all about pulling off insane manoeuvres and Closing the Gap. They run using the same Round structure as combat, with Gap resolution added at the end of each Round.

Some chases are pure pursuit. Some are races to a goal. Some are running firefights. Most are a mix, and because combat and chases use the same Rounds, you can run them together. 

### The Gap

The **Gap** represents the abstract distance between the two parties: chased (who are trying to Open the Gap) and chaser (who are trying to Close the Gap); or between racers and their goal (everyone is trying to Close the Gap first).

| Gap | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Closed** | Almost on top of each other. Boarding, ramming, and chain hooks. Short Range. |
| **Near** | Weapons range. Most manoeuvres viable. Medium Range. |
| **Far** | Pulling apart. Losing them is a real possibility. Long Range. |
| **Lost** | Chase over - the chased has escaped. |

At the end of each Round, both sides total their **Gap Closers** and **Gap Openers**. If their are net Closers, close the Gap 1 step. If their are net Openers, open the Gap 1 step. If it cancels out, don't change the Gap.

Manouevres or pure speed can open or close the Gap. Which always depends on if you're the Chaser or the Chased, the former wanting to Close the Gap and the latter to Open it.

Being **Stationary** is 1 Opener / Closer automatically.

Being **Top Speed** is 1 Closer / Opener automatically.

### Terrain Features and Hazards

The stage of a chase is never empty. Obstacles, corners and open flats whizz by at great speed.

**Features** are defined by the GM at the outset - significant landmarks and obstacles that shape the scene. A canyon pass. A collapsed overpass. A dying town. A salt flat with no cover for ten kilometres. Everyone knows these exist and can plan or react around them, with drivers in the lead directing the chase towards / through / out of features. At the end of each Round the GM states if the current feature has shifted.

**Hazards** are the fluid chaff of the chase: zipping through the scene in a blink of an eye. At the start of each Round of a Chase, the GM will note any Hazards everyone is currently hurtling towards.

GM's can use the following table to quickly generate hazards.

| 2d6 | Hazard |
|---|---|
| 2 | **Catastrophic Obstruction** - a collapsed structure, sinkhole, or burning barricade blocks the road entirely. All vehicles must manoeuvre out of the way or take d6 Damage and lose a degree of Speed. |
| 3 | **Debris Field** - large chunks of debris or rockfall scatter across the road. All vehicles must Evasive Weave or make a TN 6 roll to not take d4 Damage. |
| 4 | **Wrecked Vehicle** - a burnt-out hulk sits across the road. |
| 5 | **Tight Passage** - a narrow alleyway, canyon wall, or crumbling bridge. |
| 6 | **Slick Surface** - all manouevres will be more difficult, and spin-outs more likely. |
| 7-8 | **Clear Road** - nothing but open ground. No effect. |
| 9 | **Dust Cloud** - smoke and dust obscure vision. All ranged attacks and Perception Rolls are at a Disadvantage until the Round ends. |
| 10 | **Sharp Bend** - an unexpected turn. Vehicles at Top Speed must take a Sharp Turn or make a TN 6 roll to take another Manoeuvre. |
| 11 | **Crowd** - wastelanders, livestock, or feral loners spill across the road. Roll TN 6 to swerve past without losing a degree of Speed degree, or plow right through them. |
| 12 | **Catastrophic Hazard** - an explosion, collapsing structure, or sudden chasm. All vehicles make a TN 8 Roll or take d8 Damage and Stall. |

### Manoeuvres

Manoeuvres are special chase actions that generate Gap changes, tactical effects, or both. These are in addition to typical combat actions. GMs and Players are encouraged to think up and take note of their own manoeuvres too.

Most manoeuvres will involve Piloting Rolls - but players should use different Skills (and whatever statistic applies) if it fits their action better.

| Name | Description |
|---|---|
| **Ram** | Drive into another vehicle with intent. Opposed Roll. On a success, both vehicles take Damage: the target takes Damage equal to the attacker's Speed (Rolling = d4, Cruising = d6, Top Speed = d8). The attacker takes the same. |
| **Sideswipe** | Scrape alongside to damage or destabilise without full collision. Opposed Roll. On a success, the target takes d4 Bludgeoning Damage and their driver makes a Roll (TN 6) or Stalls. |
| **Bootleg Reverse** | A handbrake turn to quickly reverse direction and throw pursuers off. Roll vs a Hard TN of 8. On a success, your pursuers must also make a Bootleg Reverse (if they haven't committed another Action yet) or overshoot, widening the Gap by 1. Any Bootleg Reverse failure causes that vehicle to Stall. |
| **Cut Off** | When at Closed distance, you can pull in front of a target and force them to brake or swerve. Opposed Roll. On a success, the target must either lose a degree of Speed or take a Sideswipe hit. |
| **Pit Manoeuvre** | You attempt to clip the rear quarter of a target to spin it out. Opposed Roll. On a success, the target spins out losing a degree of Speed and risking crashing if any Features or Hazards are nearby. |
| **Evasive Weave** | Erratic movement making the vehicle harder to hit. Until the driver's next action, all attacks against the vehicle and its occupants are at Disadvantage. Attacks from the vehicle are also at Disadvantage. |
| **Red-Line the Engine** | You burn the engine past its rev limits, generating 1 additional Gap Closer or Opener. The engine of the vehicle takes d3 Damage (no Armour reduction). Once per Fight per vehicle. |
| **Sharp Turn** | You use the terrain to get ahead. Make a TN 4 Roll (or set by the terrain) to cut it close. On a success, generate 1 Gap Opener / Closer. On a failure, the vehicle hits the terrain, taking Damage (depending on the terrain, default d4) and lose a degree of Speed. |
| **Match Speed** | Focus entirely on maintaining the current Gap state. Opposed Roll. On a success, this counts as 2 Gap Openers / Gap Closers for the purpose of not changing the Gap. |
| **Leap** | A character makes a daring jump between two Closed moving vehicles. Take the fastest vehicle's speed for the TN. For Rolling, the TN is 4. For Cruising, it is TN 6. For Top Speed, it is TN 8. On a failure, the character falls. |
| **Drive By Shot** | Fire from atop a moving vehicle. The character has the Unstable condition unless they spend a Bonus Action to brace first. |
| **Forced Entry** | When on top an enemy vehicle, you can smash into the cabin from the outside. The TN is set by the material of the cabin. On a success, the character is inside and may immediately make a free Brawling attack against any occupant within reach. |
| **Hook and Board** | A character uses a grapple, hook, or improvised anchor to pull onto or off a vehicle. On a success, the character boards safely. On a failure, they are left dangling: Immobilised and at risk each Round. |
| **Harpoon** | A crew uses a mounted grappling hook, anchor or harpoon to tie two vehicles together. On a success, the vehicles are linked, having the same Speed. If the one in front speeds up, they both do. If the one in the back brakes, they both slow down. |
| **Deploy Trap** | Deploy caltrops, an oil slick, a burning wreck, or similar behind the vehicle. Any following vehicles must deal with this Slick Surface Hazard (in addition to other Hazards / Features). Any pursuing vehicle must make a TN 6 Roll or slide, generating a Gap Opener / Closer. |

### Notes on Core Backgrounds

A few Core Backgrounds can be re-interpretted:

**Military** - there are no armies anymore, but there were. An ex-military character served an institution that no longer exists: a soldier without a general.

**Merchant** - trade never dies. Caravans, temporary markets, and wandering salesmen exist. Currency may have gone and goods and water be all that remain, but the instinct for profit has not.

**Scholar** - rare and precious. Knowledge of the old world - engineering, medicine, science - is worth more than gold in the right settlement. Worth killing for in the wrong one.

**Criminal** - in the wasteland, law is local and often violent. A criminal is simply someone who broke the rules of wherever they came from.
